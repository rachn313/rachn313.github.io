Jan. 2018 - Dec. 2019

Wellesley Computational Chemistry Lab: Research Assistant

In order to understand cellular processes that regulate all life, we must study how molecules recognize and interact with each other in the complex cellular environment. By learning more about these important molecular interactions, scientists can develop more potent drugs that improve quality of life. Cells are highly crowded environments, with up to 40% of the cells’ total volume occupied by macromolecules. Many computational and experimental studies, however, treat biomolecular interactions as if they happen in dilute solution, without accounting for their complex environment. Studies have shown that macromolecular crowding affects molecular interactions. This study aims to create and evaluate multiple computational models for the cellular environment. The specific interacting model system we are using is Buforin II (BF2), an antimicrobial peptide (AMP) that binds to bacterial DNA, disrupting intracellular processes that are essential for cell survival. We use computational methods to study how different crowding models influence the binding between BF2 and DNA. Analyses conducted thus far show that crowding creates a more favorable interaction between BF2 and DNA. In addition to developing and evaluating models to efficiently and accurately model the complex cellular environment, our study will provide further insight into developing stronger antimicrobial peptides to combat antibiotic-resistant bacteria. I used Python, iOS terminal to store data and submit jobs to the CPU prossessers that live on campus (named Iron, Fluorine, Chlorine, and Calcium). I also learned some MATLAB features to visualize the data.

CS304: Databases with Web Interfaces Final Project: Feed

As self-proclaimed foodies, we struggle to find appetizing and aesthetic dishes at restaurants. Many social media applications lack features that make the enjoyment of sharing food photos possible. We found that Pinterest lacked information on where food photos were taken, like restaurant location or what the dish was called. Yelp contains reviews of restaurants only, and not specific dishes served at those restaurants. It lacks that appeal that instagram food shots achieve. It seemed like these social media applications all had features that were needed on one platform. As a result, Feed combines the best of social media (e.g. Instagram, Pinterest, Yelp),such as looking up dish reviews and pricing, and filtering/searching through content, searching through users and their posts. I worked on users, which included login/sign up sessions, profile page. Users can customize their profile picture (default is an avocado), change their username, email, and display name. I also worked on the followers/following system for the website. I also worked on the feature for liking/unliking posts. I also added a logout button to the navigation bar.
Learn more about Feed, checkout the technical report called readme.md
